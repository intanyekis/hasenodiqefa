Ivanka Trump Stuns in Flattering Sheer Blue Dress at W-GDP's First Anniversary Event

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Ivanka Trump looked as stylish as ever during a recent event that marked the first anniversary of her global initiative advocating for women in the workforce.

It has become a well-known fact that Trump women turn heads with their choice of clothes. This fact was proven once again during the W-GDP first Anniversary, where Ivanka Trump stunned in a blue pleated dress.

Donald Trump and Ivanka Trump attend the "The Trump Card: Playing to Win in Work and Life" book launch celebration at Trump Tower | Photo: Getty Images

Not only did she take to her social media platforms – Instagram and Twitter – to share images of her in the dress, but Daily Mail also reported that the First Daughter showed off some bright blonde highlights.

"Ivanka shares her children with Jared Kushner whom she tied the knot with, in October 2009."

While the images she shared on Twitter showed her posing with Jay Timmons as she received the National Association of Manufacturers’ inaugural “Alexander Hamilton Award,” her Instagram post featured a series of other shots.

The eye-catching garment Ivanka rocked had a mock-neck with an elegant tie, while the pleated skirt was held at the waist with elastic, which allowed Ivanka to flaunt her curves.

The Senior White House Advisor added some makeup that included long lashes, a pink lip color, and smokey eyes.

Her newly-highlighted hair was left hanging loose and straight around her shoulders in a care-free manner that made it even more attractive.

Today recalled that the mother-of-three had first switched her hairstyle up back in September 2019 when she unveiled a short bob during a trip to South America.

The site also noted that Ivanka’s hair had been gradually going through specific changes since her father, Donald Trump, became President in 2017.

As it turned out, changing her hairdo isn’t the only thing that the former model is good at as Good Housekeeping revealed that she was a dedicated and scheduled mother to her three children – Arabella Rose, Theodore James, Joseph Frederick.

Through her book, “Women Who Work,” the author mentioned that while she played with cars with Joseph for twenty minutes every day, she read at least two books a day to Arabella who loved books.

For Theodore, the working mom stated that she made sure she can give him two to three of his bottles each day before she rocked him to sleep at night.
